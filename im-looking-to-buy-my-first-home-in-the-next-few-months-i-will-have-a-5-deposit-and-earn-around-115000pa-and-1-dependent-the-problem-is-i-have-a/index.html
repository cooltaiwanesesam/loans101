Question asked by Andrew (postcode: 6172)

I'm looking to buy my first home in the next few months, I will have a 5% deposit and earn around $115,000pa and 1 dependent. The problem is I have a default on my credit history (I feel its on there by mistake) If this can't be removed will that stop me from buying a property? The amount listed was for $395
Rob Emmett - Collins Home Loans (Melbourne, 3000)
Answered 2017-09-08 00:00:00 +0000 UTC

Firstly, congratulations on embarking on the home loan journey. It is a very exciting time but I understand that issue of a default can be stressful. A small default like you describe won’t necessarily stop you from getting a home loan approved, but there are things you can do to resolve it - as it is better to go in with a clean slate. Creditors will remove defaults if you can demonstrate they are wrong or you can make arrangements with them to pay it off. Either way, the key is communication. Please feel free to contact me via my online profile to assist you further.
